# Sump Pump Pit

**Status:** Active — tripping hazard needs addressing

## Overview

Basement has a pre-dug sump pit installed during original construction. No pump was ever installed due to no water issues. The open hole is currently a tripping hazard and needs to be addressed — either covered temporarily or filled in reversibly.

## Current State

- Pit location: Rear of basement
- Pump installed: No
- Water issues to date: None
- Pit dimensions: TBD (measure before purchasing any cover)

---

## Options

### Option A — Fitted Sump Pit Cover (Recommended starting point)
Pre-made plastic lids designed specifically for sump pits. Snap or screw onto the crock rim. Airtight versions also reduce radon and humidity from the pit.

- **Pros:** Cheap ($15–40), purpose-built, easy to remove if pump ever needed, some are airtight (bonus humidity/radon benefit)
- **Cons:** Requires measuring pit diameter to get right fit (standard sizes: 15", 18", 24")
- **Where to buy:** Home Depot, Lowes, Amazon
- **Action:** Measure pit inner diameter before purchasing

### Option B — Plywood + Framing (DIY cover)
Cut a piece of ¾" plywood to sit flush over the opening, optionally framed with 2x4s to prevent shifting.

- **Pros:** Free/cheap if scrap wood on hand, customizable size, completely removable
- **Cons:** Not airtight, looks rough, can warp over time if humidity is high
- **Action:** Measure pit opening; cut to fit with ~2" overhang on all sides

### Option C — Rigid Foam Fill (Reversible)
Cut rigid foam insulation board (e.g., XPS/pink board) to fill the pit opening, sitting a few inches below grade. Cover with a piece of plywood on top.

- **Pros:** Insulates, reduces cold air and humidity from pit, fully reversible
- **Cons:** Two-step solution, foam can crumble if disturbed
- **Action:** Measure pit depth and diameter; cut foam to fit

### Option D — Fill with Pea Gravel (Semi-reversible)
Fill the pit partway with pea gravel, which is easy to scoop out if a pump is ever needed.

- **Pros:** Cheap, stable, eliminates fall hazard completely
- **Cons:** Heavy to remove if pump needed, gravel could interfere with pump installation depending on crock type
- **Not recommended** unless pit is confirmed to have a proper crock with a liner

---

## Recommendation

Start with **Option A** (fitted cover) — it's the cleanest, cheapest, purpose-built solution and keeps the pit fully accessible if you ever need a pump. Measure the pit diameter first.

---

## Notes

- Do not fill pit with concrete or permanent material — keep reversible
- Measure pit diameter before purchasing cover (standard sizes: 15", 18", 24")
