## Water Chemistry & Maintenance

Hot tub water requires more frequent attention than a pool due to smaller volume and higher temperature.

### Key Parameters to Test

| Parameter | Target Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| pH | 7.4 – 7.6 | Most critical — affects everything else |
| Total Alkalinity | 80 – 120 ppm | Buffers pH swings |
| Sanitizer (Chlorine) | 3 – 5 ppm | Or bromine: 3–5 ppm |
| Calcium Hardness | 150 – 250 ppm | Low = foamy/corrosive; high = scale |
| Total Dissolved Solids | < 1500 ppm above fill | Indicates when water needs full drain/refill |

### Routine Schedule (to be confirmed once running)

| Frequency | Task |
|-----------|------|
| After each use | Test and adjust sanitizer |
| Weekly | Full test strip or drop test (pH, alk, sanitizer, calcium) |
| Monthly | Shock treatment; rinse filter |
| Every 3–4 months | Drain, clean shell, refill with fresh water |
| Annually | Full filter replacement; inspect jets and seals |

### Sanitizer Choice

- **Chlorine:** More common, cheaper, faster acting. Can smell strong.
- **Bromine:** More stable at high temps, gentler on skin, slower acting. Common in hot tubs.
- **Salt system:** Lower maintenance, but requires a salt chlorine generator — confirm if this unit has one.

> **Action:** Confirm which sanitizer system the tub currently uses before adding any chemicals.
